Bathroom renovation FAQ: small spaces, costs, tile and lighting

How to use this FAQ for planning

This FAQ is designed to help you navigate the most common questions that arise during bathroom renovation planning. Whether you're working with a compact powder room or planning a full master suite overhaul, start by understanding your layout constraints—plumbing locations, door swings, ventilation requirements, and structural limitations. Once you've mapped those fixed elements, move to budget considerations to set realistic expectations for materials and labour.

After establishing your framework, explore finishes: tile choices, colour schemes, lighting layers, and storage configurations. This sequence ensures that your bathroom ideas are grounded in practical reality rather than aspirational images that may not suit your space or budget. Each answer below includes editorial context to help you apply the guidance to your specific situation, regardless of where you're located. The principles of good bathroom design—durability, comfort, maintainability, and calm aesthetics—are universal, even when local codes and supplier availability vary.

Common bathroom renovation questions

What are the best bathroom ideas for small spaces?

For small bathrooms, consider wall-mounted vanities, large mirrors to create the illusion of space, and walk-in showers with glass doors instead of bulky tubs.

Wall-mounted fixtures free up floor area, making the room feel less cramped and simplifying cleaning underneath. Large mirrors reflect light and visually double the perceived depth of the room. When selecting small bathroom design ideas, prioritise vertical storage—tall cabinets and shelving that draw the eye upward can make ceilings feel higher. Pocket doors or outward-opening doors can reclaim valuable floor space that a traditional inward swing would consume. Light colour palettes and consistent tile from floor to wall reduce visual breaks that can make a small space feel fragmented.

How much does a typical bathroom renovation cost?

A bathroom renovation can range from $5,000 for basic updates to $30,000 or more for a full remodel, depending on materials, layout changes, and fixtures chosen.

Budget-conscious updates might include repainting, replacing hardware, upgrading lighting, and installing a new vanity without moving plumbing. Mid-range renovations often involve new tile, a walk-in shower installation, and improved ventilation, typically falling between $12,000 and $20,000. High-end remodels with layout changes, luxury materials, underfloor heating, and custom cabinetry can exceed $30,000. Labour often represents 40–50% of total costs, so understanding what you can reasonably do yourself versus what requires licensed trades will significantly impact your final spend. Always include a contingency of 10–15% for unexpected issues like hidden water damage or outdated wiring.

What bathroom tile inspiration is trending right now?

Large-format tiles, zellige-style handmade tiles, and bold geometric patterns are currently popular, along with natural stone looks and terrazzo-inspired designs.

Large-format tiles (60×60 cm or larger) minimise grout lines, creating a cleaner, more expansive look that suits modern aesthetics and simplifies maintenance. Zellige tiles bring artisan character with their irregular glazes and slight dimensional variations, ideal for feature walls or niches. Geometric patterns—hexagons, chevrons, or encaustic-look porcelain—add visual interest without overwhelming small spaces when used strategically. Bathroom tile inspiration should balance aesthetics with practicality: consider slip resistance for wet areas, ease of cleaning, and how the tile scale interacts with your room dimensions. Terrazzo-inspired designs offer a playful, speckled texture that hides minor staining and pairs well with neutral bathroom color schemes.

How to choose the right bathroom lighting design?

Layer your bathroom lighting with ambient ceiling lights, task lighting around the vanity at face level, and accent lighting for architectural features to create both functionality and atmosphere.

Ambient lighting provides overall illumination—recessed ceiling fixtures or a flush-mount fitting work well. Task lighting is critical at the vanity: wall-mounted sconces on either side of the mirror at eye level (roughly 150–165 cm from the floor) eliminate shadows on the face, far superior to a single overhead fixture. Accent lighting can highlight a textured tile wall, a freestanding tub, or open shelving. Choose bulbs with a colour temperature between 2700K and 3000K for a warm, flattering glow, or 3500K–4000K if you prefer a crisper, more clinical light for grooming tasks. Dimmers add flexibility, and ensuring fixtures have appropriate moisture ratings for bathroom zones will extend their lifespan and maintain safety. Good bathroom lighting design transforms both function and mood.

What are the best bathroom storage solutions for a clutter-free space?

Recessed medicine cabinets, floating shelves, vanity drawer organizers, and built-in shower niches are excellent ways to maximize storage without sacrificing style.

Recessed cabinets sit flush with the wall, saving space while providing concealed storage for toiletries and medications. Floating shelves offer open display for decorative items or frequently used products, but require regular tidying to avoid visual clutter. Drawer organisers—dividers, trays, and tiered inserts—keep small items like cosmetics and grooming tools accessible and orderly. Built-in shower niches eliminate the need for hanging caddies and can be tiled to match the surrounding walls for a seamless look. Vertical storage, such as tall linen cabinets or over-toilet shelving units, makes use of often-wasted space. Thoughtful bathroom storage solutions support daily routines, reduce countertop clutter, and contribute to the calm, uncluttered aesthetic that makes a bathroom feel like a retreat.

Why are walk-in shower designs so popular?

Walk-in showers offer a sleek, modern aesthetic, improve accessibility for all ages, make bathrooms feel more spacious, and are easier to clean than traditional shower-tub combinations.

The absence of a tub surround or shower curtain creates an open, airy feel, particularly valuable in smaller bathrooms. A curbless or low-threshold entry improves accessibility for children, older adults, and anyone with mobility challenges, aligning with universal design principles. Glass panels or screens contain water while maintaining sightlines, visually expanding the room. Walk in shower designs also simplify cleaning—fewer joints, no curtain to launder, and large-format tiles mean less grout to scrub. When planning a walk-in shower, ensure adequate floor drainage, appropriate slope (typically 1:50 to 1:80), and waterproofing that extends well beyond the wet area. Pair with a handheld showerhead on a slide bar for maximum flexibility.
